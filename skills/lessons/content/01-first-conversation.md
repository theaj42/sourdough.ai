## Part 2: How This Actually Works

Here's what just happened when you asked your question:

1. **You asked** in natural language (no special format needed)
2. **I understood** the intent (what you actually wanted)
3. **I took action** (read a file, searched the web, explained something)
4. **I responded** with results

This is the whole pattern. Everything else builds on this.

### What Makes Questions Work Well

**Specific questions work better**:
- ‚úÖ "Can you read the README file in my sourdough.ai directory?"
- ‚úÖ "Show me what's in my ai-data folder"
- ‚úÖ "Help me understand what session logging means"

**Vague questions are harder**:

## Part 3: Understanding What AI Can Do

Let me show you what's actually possible in this system.

### I Can:
- **Read files** you point me to
- **Write new files** when you need them created
- **Edit existing files** with precise changes
- **Run commands** (bash on Mac/Linux, PowerShell on Windows)
- **Search the web** for information
- **Remember context** from our conversation
- **Explain concepts** and answer questions

### I Can't:
- **Read your mind** (please be specific!)
- **Access files** without you telling me to
- **Know things** you haven't told me
- **Remember past conversations** automatically (unless we're logging‚Äîyou'll learn about that in Lesson 10)
- **Make perfect decisions** without your guidance

### The Key Philosophy

This isn't about AI replacing you. It's about **AI augmenting you**.

Think of it like this:
- **You're the chef** - you decide what to make
- **I'm your sous chef** - I help with the prep work
- **Together we cook faster** - but you're still in charge

### Common Questions

Before we finish, here are answers to questions new users often have:

**"How do you actually work?"**
I'm an AI language model that can use tools (read files, run commands, search the web). When you ask me to do something, I figure out which tools to use and how to use them.

**"Is my data private?"**
Yes. Everything stays on your local machine unless you explicitly ask me to reach out to the web. I can't access anything you don't point me to.

**"What if you make a mistake?"**
You can always undo changes. And I'll help you fix anything that goes wrong. That's what the next lessons are about‚Äîdoing things safely.
